Every Thing Is Sacred

To be Christian is to see Christ in every thing. The book "The Universal Christ" by Richard Rohr reminds us that the spirit of Christ is alive in all living flesh.

I saw ... the reverence that everyone must have for a sinner; instead of condoning [their] sin, which in reality is [their] utmost sorrow, one must comfort Christ who is suffering in [them]. And this reverence must be paid even to the those sinners whose souls seem to be dead, because it is Christ, who is the life of the soul, who is dead in them; they are His tombs, and Christ in the tomb [has the potential to become] the risen Christ. . . . Christ is everywhere; in Him every kind of life has a meaning and has an influence on every other kind of life.

— Caryll Houselander, A Rocking-Horse Catholic

Apply no violence and beware of shedding blood

The human body and the soul, which is from God and lives in the human body, are the house of God, the temple of the Holy Spirit. Deep in the soul is God's love, wisdom, and justice. Thus, God dwells in the soul. Accordingly, the soul and body of the human being are the temple of God. And so, the one who breaks into the house of flesh and bone, by doing violence to people, by subjugating them, by treating them as slaves, or even killing them, sins against the Holy Spirit. ... So keep your hands pure and your soul pure, not just from theft and robbery. But above all, do not use violence, be it on people on animals, and beware of shedding their blood.
